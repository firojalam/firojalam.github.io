---

## Why this matters

As AI-driven content moderation and misinformation detection systems become widespread, many still struggle with **cultural grounding and ethical context**, particularly for non-Western societies. Generic approaches risk overlooking culturally specific norms of truth, responsibility, and dialogue.

Our work aims to bridge this gap by integrating **Islamic media ethics** such as truthfulness, justice, and accountability in communication with **state-of-the-art AI and NLP**, enabling more **responsible, culturally aware, and trustworthy digital technologies**.

---

## Research team and collaboration

The project brings together an interdisciplinary team across **HBKU, QCRI and Doha Institute for Graduate Studies**, combining expertise in:
- Artificial Intelligence and Arabic NLP  
- Islamic studies and media ethics  
- Public policy and cross-cultural communication  

---

## What we are building

Rather than focusing solely on content filtering, the project will develop AI-powered solutions that support:
- **Context-aware fact-checking**
- **Ethically grounded content moderation**
- **Constructive and dialogical cross-cultural engagement**

Key outcomes will include **benchmarking datasets, evaluation frameworks, and AI models** grounded in Islamic sources, international law, and human rights principles, alongside **digital literacy tools** and **AI-enhanced dialogue platforms**.
